<p><p>The pharmaceutical propellants market includes two main types: liquefied gases and compressed gases. Liquefied gases, such as hydrofluoroalkanes (HFAs), are stored in liquid form and release as gas when pressure is reduced, providing controlled delivery in inhalers. Compressed gases, like nitrogen and carbon dioxide, remain gaseous under pressure and are used for various applications, including aerosol medication delivery. Both types play crucial roles in the efficiency and effectiveness of pharmaceutical delivery systems, ensuring accurate dosage and optimal patient outcomes.</p></p>
