<p><p>The artificial colors market is divided into two primary types: powder and granule. Powdered artificial colors are finely milled, providing a concentrated color that easily disperses in various applications like food, cosmetics, and pharmaceuticals. Granular artificial colors, on the other hand, are coarser in texture, allowing for controlled dosing and sometimes better stability in formulations. Both types serve various industries, catering to specific needs such as solubility, shelf life, and application methods, enhancing the visual appeal of products.</p></p>
