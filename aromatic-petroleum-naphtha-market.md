<p><p>Aromatic petroleum naphtha is divided into two main market types: heavy naphtha and light naphtha. Heavy naphtha, with a higher boiling point and density, is primarily used in producing gasoline and petrochemicals. It serves as a crucial feedstock for reforming processes and as a solvent. Light naphtha, on the other hand, has a lower boiling point and is often utilized as a solvent in industrial applications or as a blending component for gasoline production, enhancing octane ratings and performance.</p></p>
