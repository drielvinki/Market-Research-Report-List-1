<p><p>Virtual Machines (VMs) are software-based emulations of physical computers, enabling multiple operating systems to run concurrently on a single hardware platform. The market for VMs can be divided into two main types: System Virtual Machines, which replicate entire hardware systems to host multiple OS environments, and Process Virtual Machines, designed to execute specific applications by providing a platform-independent environment. Both types enhance resource utilization, streamline development, and facilitate testing, contributing significantly to the efficiency and flexibility of IT infrastructure.</p></p>
